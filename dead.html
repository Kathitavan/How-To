Death Certificate Information

1. Required Documents

You need to provide the following documents:

  • Proof of Death (e.g., Hospital Discharge Summary, Death Report)
  • Proof of Identity of the Deceased (e.g., Aadhar Card, Passport, Voter ID)
  • Proof of Identity of the Informant (e.g., Aadhar Card, Passport, Voter ID)
  • Proof of Relationship (e.g., Marriage Certificate, Family Register)

2. Application Process

The steps to apply for a Death Certificate:

  • Visit the local municipal office or online portal.
  • Complete the application form for a death certificate.
  • Submit the required documents.
  • Pay the application fee if applicable.
  • Receive the certificate once the application is processed.

3. Eligibility

Eligibility requirements may vary by jurisdiction, but generally, you must be a relative or legal representative of the deceased.

4. Cost

The cost of obtaining a death certificate varies by location. Check with your local authority for specific fees.

5. Tracking

You can track the status of your application through the local municipal office or their online portal.
